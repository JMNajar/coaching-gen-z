Cue Quick-Start
30 Pole Vault Coaching Cues — Plain Language — Immediate Application
From Coaching Generation Z Athletes by Kent Rhodes  ·  jessup.edu Pole Vault Program

The most important coaching principle in this guide:

“One cue per rep. If the athlete can’t act on it immediately, it’s not effective communication.”

Section 1
Approach & Run-Up
Avoid This Say This Visualization Targets
"Run faster" "Accelerate through your marks" "Like a jet building speed down the runway — smooth, then explosive" Consistent acceleration, not top-end speed
"Be more aggressive" "Tall spine, drive your knees" "Imagine a flagpole running from the ground through the top of your head" Posture and knee drive, not raw energy
"Hit your steps" "Trust your checkmarks — don’t look down" "Eyes on the box, not the ground" Eyes forward, confidence in the approach
"Speed up at the end" "Last three strides: penultimate is your power step" "Coil and explode — like a spring releasing" Penultimate step as the power generation point
"Stay in control" "Commit to the approach; the pole handles the rest" "You’re setting up the system — let it work" Mental commitment, reduces hesitation
Section 2
Plant & Takeoff
Avoid ThisSay ThisVisualizationTargets
"Plant harder" "Left hand leads, right hand punches sky" "Like throwing a spear straight up — both hands active" Hand position and upward drive on plant
"Jump higher" "Drive up and through — not at the bar" "Launch like a rocket, not a standing jump" Vertical drive, not horizontal stalling
"Get on the pole" "Plant tall — left arm high, eyes up" "Like raising a flag with both arms extended" Body position at the moment of plant
"Wait longer" "Plant at your last stride, not before it" "The plant happens when your foot hits — same moment" Plant timing with takeoff foot
"More power" "One word at takeoff: UP" "Gravity reversed — your whole body going vertical" Simplifies the cue to single direction focus
Section 3
Swing & Inversion
Avoid ThisSay ThisVisualizationTargets
"Pull yourself up" "Swing long, swing fast — bottom hand anchors" "Like a pendulum swinging from your bottom hand" Long free swing, not an early pull
"Curl up" "Hips to sky — lead with your feet" "Your feet are a rocket and the pole is the launch pad" Hip drive and feet-first inversion
"Stay tight" "Long body on the swing, then compress at the top" "A spring stretching out before it coils" Full extension before compression at inversion
"Kick over" "Drive your toes to the sky — don’t curl" "Toes pointing straight up at the top of your swing" Clean inversion, toes leading not curling
"Get upside down" "Let the pole carry you — your job is the swing" "You’re a passenger until the top — then you drive" Trust the pole, focus on swing mechanics
Section 4
Turn, Extension & Push-Off
Avoid ThisSay ThisVisualizationTargets
"Turn faster" "Hips rotate — shoulder follows" "Like a wheel rotating: hips are the axle" Hip-initiated rotation, not shoulder-led
"Push off the pole" "Extend fully, then push from your shoulders" "Full stretch first — like pushing off a wall when fully extended" Full extension before push initiates
"Get away from the pole" "Release when your hips are above your hands" "Let go at the peak — not before, not after" Release timing at optimal point
"Keep pushing" "One push — clean and complete" "Like a clean bench press — full extension, then release" Single clean push-off, not multiple attempts
"Don’t drop your legs" "Hips stay high through the extension" "Body stays as vertical as possible until release" Hip height maintained through push phase
Section 5
Bar Clearance & Flight
Avoid ThisSay ThisVisualizationTargets
"Don’t knock the bar" "Eyes to the sky — arch and reach" "Like a bridge between the standards — belly up, back arched" Focus on body position, not the bar
"Pull your feet over" "Chest up and through first, legs follow" "Lead with your chest, let legs trail like a ribbon" Sequential clearance: upper body leads
"Be careful at the top" "Commit to the clearance — arch and reach" "You’re already above it — just complete the position" Confidence through bar clearance
"Watch the bar" "Eyes on the sky until you land" "Look for clouds, not the crossbar" Gaze direction prevents premature collapse
"Stay tight" "Long body over the bar — fully extended" "Like a diver in a layout position over the water" Full extension at bar for maximum clearance height
Section 6
General Reset & Pressure Cues
SituationCueWhy It Works
Athlete in their head before a rep "One thing. What’s your one thing?" Forces focus to a single technical target; eliminates overload
Athlete rattled after a miss "Breathe first. Vault second." Breaks the emotional spiral, resets physiological state
Competition nerves, overthinking "You’ve done this a thousand times. Just do it again." Reconnects athlete to practiced skill, not novel pressure
Athlete losing form under pressure "Back to the approach — that’s where it starts" Returns focus to the controllable beginning, not the outcome
Athlete focusing on result "Process, not outcome. What’s the process?" Redirects from bar height anxiety to technical execution

The Reset Protocol — When an Athlete Is Spiraling

1
Simplify
Strip to one cue. One technical focus only.
2
Stabilize
Lower the bar. Rebuild the rep count at manageable height.
3
Rebuild
Re-introduce complexity and height only after confidence returns.
