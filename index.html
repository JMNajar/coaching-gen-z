Experience the voice of the book before you buy. The introduction sets up everything that follows.

Coaching has changed. Not because athletes are worse — but because they are different.

The athletes you’re working with today grew up during a global pandemic. They spent their developmental years separated from competition, isolated from structured team environments, and cut off from the daily rhythm of organized sport. When they came back, they didn’t just return to the game. They returned with gaps — social, emotional, and developmental gaps that no conditioning program can address.

That’s the problem. And it’s why the old approach isn’t working.

Old-school coaching assumed that intensity drives performance. Get in their face. Push harder. Make them uncomfortable until they respond. That model was built for a different athlete. Today’s athlete doesn’t lack effort. They lack certainty. They don’t need more pressure. They need clarity.

This book is built around three principles that work with today’s athlete — not against them: Clarity. Connection. Consistency. Not as soft alternatives to accountability. As the most effective tools a modern coach can carry.

Don’t coach louder. Don’t coach harder. Coach clearer.

About the Author

Kent Rhodes

KR
Kent Rhodes
Pole Vault Coach — Jessup University

Kent Rhodes has spent more than 25 years developing athletes at the high school and collegiate levels. Known for his direct communication style, technical consistency, and athlete-centered approach, Rhodes has worked with vaulters from beginners to elite state-level competitors throughout Northern California.

Rhodes currently serves as Volunteer Assistant Coach for the pole vault program at Jessup University, where he has helped develop NAIA All-Americans and conference champions. He has also coached at Placer High School, Del Oro High School, and Pleasant Grove — while operating a year-round private pole vault training program serving athletes across the region.

Over his career, Rhodes has coached more than 20 athletes to the CIF State Championships. His work is focused not only on technical improvement, but on the communication, accountability, and confidence-building that turns potential into performance.
