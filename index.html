About This Site

Five field-ready tools —
built around the book.

This dashboard is a practical companion to Coaching Generation Z Athletes. Every tool is designed to be used on the sideline — fast to pull up, easy to apply, and built around the core principle of the book: the simpler the message, the faster athletes improve.

Tool 01
Coaching Cue Library
Every “say this / avoid this” line from the book. Searchable and filterable by situation, athlete type, and scenario.
Tool 02
Athlete Archetype Guide
Identify which of the five Gen Z athlete types you’re working with — and get the right response immediately.
Tool 03
The Reset Protocol
Simplify → Stabilize → Rebuild. A fast visual reference for when an athlete is spiraling under pressure.
Tool 04
Goal Setting Tool
Turn vague goals into Specific, Measurable, Controllable targets. Generates a printable goal card for each athlete.
Tool 05
Practice Planner
Build your session around one focus per rep. Generates a clean practice card before you step on the track.
About the Author

Kent Rhodes

KR
Kent Rhodes
Pole Vault Coach — Jessup University

Jessup University pole vault coach Kent Rhodes has spent more than 25 years developing athletes in the pole vault at the high school and collegiate levels. Known for his direct communication style, technical consistency, and athlete-centered coaching approach, Rhodes has worked with vaulters ranging from beginners to elite state-level competitors throughout Northern California.

Rhodes currently serves as Volunteer Assistant Coach for the pole vault program at Jessup University, where he has helped develop nationally competitive athletes and NAIA All-Americans. Since joining the program, Jessup pole vaulters have earned conference championships, national-level performances, and All-American honors.

In addition to collegiate coaching, Rhodes has coached at Placer High School, Del Oro High School, and currently at Pleasant Grove — while also operating a year-round private pole vault training program serving athletes across Northern California. Over the years, he has coached numerous CIF Section, Masters, and State Meet qualifiers, including more than 20 athletes who advanced to the CIF State Championships.

Rhodes is recognized for simplifying technical instruction and helping athletes compete with confidence under pressure. His coaching philosophy emphasizes:

Clarity
Consistency
Athlete Trust
Process-Based Development

His work focuses not only on technical improvement, but also on communication, accountability, confidence building, and long-term athlete development.

Rhodes is the author of Coaching Generation Z Athletes: A Guide for Modern Sports Leaders — a practical coaching guide focused on modern athlete communication, motivation, and performance development.
