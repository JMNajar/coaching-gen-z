The Reset Protocol
Deploy when an athlete is spiraling. Reduce first. Rebuild from there.
Trigger Signs — When to Deploy
Hesitation
Emotional withdrawal
Frustration
Inconsistency spikes
Shoulders tighten
Eye contact drops
Shutdown behavior
Silence
1
Simplify
Cut all technical information. Give the athlete one thing and only one thing.
  • "Reset."
  • "One focus."
  • "First three steps only."
  • "Hit your mid-mark."
→
2
Stabilize
Let emotions settle. Calm the environment before adding anything back.
  • "Take a breath."
  • "We're resetting the rhythm."
  • "Same run, same takeoff."
  • "Clean rhythm."
→
3
Rebuild
Reintroduce one controllable action. Build from a small win first.
  • "Give me one clean rep."
  • "Repeat that execution."
  • "Today we only care about approach rhythm."
  • "One focus. Let's go."
What NOT to do during a spiral
✗ Add more technical corrections
✗ Raise your voice
✗ Show frustration or disappointment
✗ Say “You’re overthinking” or “Relax”
✗ Stack cues — even well-intentioned ones add overload
“The worse an athlete feels, the simpler coaching must become. Overloaded athletes cannot process multiple corrections. The best coaches reduce information instead of increasing it.”
