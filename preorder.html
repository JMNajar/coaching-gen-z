Coaching Generation Z Athletes

A Guide for Modern Sports Leaders
By Kent Rhodes — Pole Vault Coach, Jessup University

Today’s athletes aren’t broken — they’re different. After years of developmental disruption, they came back to sport with gaps that intensity alone can’t fix. This book gives coaches a practical, field-tested system for reaching, motivating, and developing the Gen Z athlete at every level.

What’s Inside

14 chapters. One system.
Built for coaches who want results.

Chapter 1
The COVID Effect
What athletes lost during the pandemic years — and why those gaps are still showing up at practice.
Chapter 2
The Information-Overloaded Athlete
“Today’s athlete processes more than any generation before. What they lack isn’t effort — it’s certainty.”
Chapter 3
Why Old-School Is Breaking Down
Intensity vs. clarity. Why the approach that worked for previous generations is failing today.
Chapter 4
What Gen Z Actually Wants
“They don’t want less coaching. They want better coaching.” What that looks like in practice.
Chapter 5
Connection Before Correction
The sequence that builds trust: Connect → Correct → Confirm. Why order matters.
Chapter 6
The One-Cue Rule
“If the athlete can’t act on it immediately, it’s not effective communication.”
Chapter 7
Accountability That Sticks
Moving athletes from emotional accountability to structured accountability — without confrontation.
Chapter 8
Motivation Follows Execution
Why pre-practice speeches don’t work — and what actually builds drive in a Gen Z athlete.
Chapter 9
The Athlete Who Shuts Down
“Shutdown is not defiance. It’s overload.” How to recognize it and what to do.
Chapter 10
The Resistant Athlete
“Resistance is not the problem. It’s a signal.” What it’s telling you — and how to respond.
Chapter 11
No Confidence
“Confidence is not something you say. It’s something you build.” The step-by-step process.
Chapter 12
Parents, Pressure & Outside Noise
“You are filtering noise.” How to manage external pressure and protect your athlete’s focus.
Chapter 13
Building Team Culture
“What you allow becomes the standard. What you reinforce becomes the culture.”
Chapter 14
The Social Media Trap
“Comparison destroys confidence. Execution builds it.” How to coach athletes in the comparison era.
