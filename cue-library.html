Coaching Cue Library

46 short, repeatable cues pulled from Coaching Generation Z Athletes — what to say, what to avoid, and the exact situation each one is built for. Search by situation or filter by category.

Athlete visibly frustrated or spiraling

“Eyes up.”

Competition or high-pressure moment

Body mechanics cue — short and actionable

Rebuilding confidence mid-session

✗ Avoid This
✗
“What are you doing?”
Creates shame — athlete shuts down or gets defensive
✗
“You should know better.”
Emotional — destroys trust, creates fear
✗
“Focus.”
Vague — athlete is already trying, this adds nothing
✗
“Want it more.”
Emotional appeal — not actionable
✗
“Try harder.”
Vague — no clear action, creates frustration
✗
“Do exactly what I say.”
Creates compliance, not ownership or buy-in
✗
“Stop doing that.”
No replacement instruction — athlete left without guidance
✗
“Fix everything.”
Overloads athlete immediately — opposite of what's needed
✗
“Be more consistent.”
Outcome-focused, not process — athlete doesn't know how
✗
“Relax.”
Dismissive — athletes cannot execute this instruction
✗
“You're overthinking.”
Labels the problem without providing a solution
✗
“Wake up.”
Shaming a distracted athlete — increases withdrawal
✗
“Get fired up.”
Emotional, not executable — athletes don't know how to do this
✗
“Bring energy.”
Vague — athlete doesn't know what action to take
✗
“That's not good enough.”
Shame-based — no path forward given
✗
“You're not trying.”
Almost always wrong — usually overload, not effort
✗
“We need a big jump.”
Outcome focus increases pressure at the worst moment
✗
“Fix your run, stay tall, and get up earlier.”
Three corrections at once — guaranteed overload
