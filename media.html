About Kent Rhodes

KR
Kent Rhodes
Author — Pole Vault Coach, Jessup University — Speaker

Kent Rhodes is a pole vault coach and author who has spent more than 25 years developing athletes at the high school and collegiate levels in Northern California. Currently serving as Volunteer Assistant Coach at Jessup University — where he has helped develop NAIA All-Americans and conference champions — Rhodes has also coached at Placer High School, Del Oro High School, and Pleasant Grove, and operates a year-round private training program serving athletes across the region.

His book, Coaching Generation Z Athletes: A Guide for Modern Sports Leaders, draws on decades of frontline coaching experience to address the single most pressing challenge in modern athletics: how do you reach, motivate, and develop an athlete shaped by a global pandemic, social media, and constant information overload? Rhodes’s answer is built on three principles — Clarity, Connection, and Consistency — and delivers a practical system coaches can apply immediately.

Suggested Topics

Five talking points for any format.

Topic 1 — The COVID Effect
What coaches are still dealing with from the pandemic years
Athletes who were in middle school during 2020–21 missed critical developmental windows — not just conditioning, but the social and emotional structure that sport normally builds automatically. They returned to competition carrying gaps that intensity alone can’t fix. Kent explains exactly what was lost and what coaches can do about it now.
“You can’t discipline your way out of a development deficit.”
Topic 2 — Why Old-School Is Breaking Down
The approach that worked for previous generations is failing today’s athlete
Old-school coaching was built on one premise: intensity drives performance. But today’s athlete doesn’t lack effort — they lack certainty. Pushing harder creates shutdown, not output. Kent walks through the specific reasons why, and what coaches who are struggling with today’s athletes are likely doing wrong without knowing it.
“Don’t coach louder. Don’t coach harder. Coach clearer.”
Topic 3 — The Five Athlete Archetypes
Every Gen Z coach encounters the same five athlete types — most coaches only know how to handle one
The athlete who shuts down. The resistant athlete. The athlete with no confidence. The athlete crushed by parent pressure. The athlete who can’t find motivation. Kent profiles each archetype, explains what’s actually driving the behavior, and gives coaches a specific, practical response for each one.
“Shutdown is not defiance. It’s overload. Resistance is not the problem — it’s a signal.”
Topic 4 — The One-Cue Rule
The single coaching habit that changes everything
Most coaches give athletes too much information per rep — corrections, reminders, encouragement, and criticism all at once. The result is paralysis, not performance. Kent’s one-cue rule is simple, research-grounded, and immediately applicable: one cue per rep. If the athlete can’t act on it immediately, it’s not effective communication.
“Say less. Be clearer.”
Topic 5 — Building Culture with Gen Z
How team culture actually forms with today’s athlete — and what kills it
Social media comparison, parent pressure, motivation deficits, and inconsistent standards all erode culture before it can take root. Kent explains the two-part formula that works with Gen Z: what you allow becomes the standard, and what you reinforce becomes the culture. Coaches leave with a clear understanding of where their culture is built — and where it’s quietly being destroyed.
“Comparison destroys confidence. Execution builds it.”
